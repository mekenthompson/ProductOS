# Formulation Review: [Segment / domain] ([Date])

> Product reviews critique the **solution**. Post-launch reviews critique the **outcome**. This forum critiques the **understanding**: the [formulation](./customer-formulation.md) itself. It borrows two mechanisms: the clinician's case supervision (even veterans present their formulations to peers, because the known failure mode is over-attachment to your own) and the courtroom (someone's explicit job is to expose what you don't know).

---

## Rules of engagement

1. **Attack the formulation, not the person.** The presenter's job is to defend with evidence or concede; conceding is a win for the forum.
2. **Opposing counsel prepares.** Reads the formulation and its evidence base in advance and arrives with specific challenges, not vibes.
3. **Evidence outranks seniority.** A line in the formulation survives on its evidence links, not on who wrote it.
4. **Every review draws blood.** If the formulation leaves unchanged, the review failed: either the attack was soft or the formulation is stale. Something is revised, downgraded to `[GUESS]`, or added to known unknowns, every time.

---

## The attack lines

Opposing counsel works through these; attendees pile on.

- **Observation vs interpretation.** Pick two claims. Is each an observed behaviour or an interpretation dressed as one? What's the source?
- **Single-source test.** Which load-bearing claims rest on one source? Whose agenda was that source carrying?
- **The economics test.** Presenter sketches the customer's economics from memory, live. The domain veteran in the room grades it.
- **Three-real-customers test.** Name three actual accounts this formulation describes. Does it predict their last three decisions? Where does it fail?
- **The assumption most likely wrong.** Presenter must nominate one, unprompted. Then the room nominates theirs. If they differ, that gap is the finding.
- **Disconfirming evidence.** What has the presenter seen in the last quarter that *cuts against* this formulation? "Nothing" is not an acceptable answer; it means they've stopped looking.
- **Going-native check.** Which of the customer's own assumptions has the presenter absorbed? What would an outsider from another industry question about how this job is done at all?
- **Cross-craft consistency.** Do the sections agree? If the PM's economics say the operator is judged on throughput but design's acceptance edge says they resist any workflow change, one of them is wrong, or the tension itself is the finding. Attacking another craft's section is the system working.
