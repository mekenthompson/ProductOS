├── jobs/

    └── <change>.md        ship-coupled delivery, references a Job Spec

This is the hierarchy you write into your own repo. Nothing to install, no
service to run, just markdown files that sit next to your code.

## Building got cheap. Knowing what to build got scarce.

An agent can draft the spec, write the code, and run the tests before you've
booked the kickoff. The bottleneck moved. It isn't capacity any more.

Product is two things: domain expertise and product discipline. Domain
expertise is knowing your customers' jobs well enough to say what is worth
building and why. Discipline is the mechanics of shipping the right stuff,
properly. Agents now do the mechanics cheaply, so expertise is the leg that
matters more, and it matters more every year.

ProductOS is how that expertise stays in the loop. Each layer of the hierarchy
above gets its own document, so the judgement behind every decision carries
down the chain while the work runs at agent speed.

## One rule decides what ships

<Aside type="tip" title="The verdict rule">
A change ships only when it advances a vision outcome, satisfies its Job Spec,
passes every principle, and crosses no invariant.
</Aside>

Agents build fast inside those gates. Humans own the gates.

The rule is the bar, not the paperwork. Scale the gates to the stakes: a quick
win ships on a Tech Lead's nod, a payments change runs every gate at full depth.
One path for everything is no path at all.

| | Quick Win | Lightweight | Full Spec |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Size** | ≤ half a day | ½ day-2 wks | > 2 wks |
| **Process** | Flag Tech Lead/PM, ship | 1-page brief | Full RFC + RICE |
| **Gates** | Outcome sanity check | Outcome UAT + 2-wk review | Full verdict, 2wk/30d/90d |

## The right context, for whoever's deciding

Product isn't one decision. It's a chain of them, at different altitudes.
ProductOS gives each layer its own document, so whoever's working there, a PM,
an engineer, a designer, PMM, or an agent, has exactly the context that layer
needs and nothing it doesn't.

## Adopt it

There's no installer. You adopt ProductOS by writing these five things into
your own repo.

1. **Read the method**, [Agentic Delivery](/guides/agentic-delivery/): the four parts and the verdict rule.
2. **Copy the shapes**: the templates for [anchors](/anchors/product-vision/), [product spec](/templates/product-spec/), and [Job Spec](/templates/job-spec/).
3. **Write your anchors**: [vision](/anchors/product-vision/), [principles](/anchors/product-principles/), [invariants](/anchors/invariants/). The why, the built-well, the never-cross.
4. **Write your product spec and first Job Spec**, using the [create-product-spec](/skills/create-product-spec/) and [create-job-spec](/skills/create-job-spec/) skills to interview you and write them.
5. **Point your agent at your AGENTS.md** so it reads the same documents as executable guidance: drafting RFCs with [create-rfc](/skills/create-rfc/), gating every document through [review-doc](/skills/review-doc/), and guarding ratified specs with [amend-durable-doc](/skills/amend-durable-doc/).
